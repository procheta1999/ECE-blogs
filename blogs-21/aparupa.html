Photo-Biomodulation


Aparupa Chatterjee

The term ‘Photo-biomodulation’ was first introduced in 1997. Previously it was known as ‘Low Level Laser Therapy’. It was first developed in 1967 by a Hungarian doctor Endre Mester. There are two basic roots in which light can be used to cure disease – one is to combine the light with photosensitizing dyes in what we call photodynamic therapy which may cause harm to the cells and the other is to use the light alone, which is known as low level laser therapy. It tends to simulate things.

History:

In 1967, Endre Mester wondered if lasers can actually cause cancer irrespective of any situation. So, he wanted to pull out some theories. He took some mice, shaved their back hair and made two groups- one group got applied with the low powered ruby laser to one group and another was unchanged. Instead of getting cancer, the laser treated group got their hair back more quickly than the others. This is how the low laser therapy started.

During the early years, this laser biomodulation process encountered some problems. There were some terms such as Bio stimulation, Cool Laser, Soft Laser or Soft Power Laser Therapy. In 2015 the term 'Photobiomodulation’ or ‘Photobiomodulation Therapy’ was added as a new term to the existing record of ‘Low Level Laser Therapy’ because of the efforts of Dr. Praveen Arany. People tend to use low level energy because it works better than the high levels Which is the whole concept of biphasic dose response or hormesis.

Treatment:

In general, a few joules per square centimeter of red or near infrared light will be enough to cure the problem but a large amount such as 5000 joules per square will be detrimental. These good effects will go away and there is a chance that the detrimental effects may go away. This involves dissecting the molecular and cellular mechanisms in which the photons are absorbed by the mitochondria. They stimulate more ATP, produce low levels of oxygens and activate transcription factors such as NF kappa-b which is a very clear trophic transcription factor and cause the translation of many gene products that do all the beneficial things of low-level life so what could be produced at low levels.

A light source is placed near the skin or touching the skin, allowing the photons of light to penetrate tissue where it interacts with chromophores of cells. It results in photochemical changes that lead to changes in cells and tissues of the body. The main mechanisms occur at mitochondria. Light induces a complex chain of reactions in damaged tissues which fastens the wound healing or the cell reformation. It helps in restoring the normal cellular function. Well, a recent paper says that light can enhance the performance of tissues and cells. The effect depends on the density of the light and for how much time it has been applied. Usually, the application time is between 30-60 seconds.

It is a non-thermal process involving endogenous chromophores obtaining photochemical events at various biological levels. PBM devices have been lately cleared for marketing by FDA through the Premarket Notification/510(k) process as sub-essential devices for the temporary relief of pain.

Application:

This technology is used to stimulate wound healing. In basic cases or to be accurate minor such as aches or scrapes it can stimulate healing of fractured bone or tendon ruptures or cartilage. It is even used for serious injuries such as spinal cord injuries, traumatic brain injuries or heart attacks or strokes. It produces inflammation and reduces pain. PBMT has assured cure from many sports injuries such as hamstring pulls, plantar fasciitis. The United States and many other Olympic teams have adopted it as essential pain management.

Contradictions:

Though PBMT is used to relieve lots of problems but there are some exceptions also.
1. This infra-red light may cause harm if viewed from a distance less than 1.1 meters.
2. It shouldn’t be used over any known malignant reasons such as cancer therapy. It may cause side effects: oral mucositis, radiation dermatitis.
3. Treatment over a tattoo may cause unbearable pain because of the laser heat.
