An Insight into DevSecOps


Prantap Nag

The unfortunate events of 2020 such as the pandemic popularized the “work from home “culture. This led to the acceleration and adoption of the cloud-based business models. The cloud along with containerization technology offers highly scalable, user-friendly services which have greatly aided to the “work from home “culture. With the rapid increase in the use of such services in recent times, the security now demands immediate importance. This is where DevSecOps comes into play.


What is DevSecOps?

DevSecOps stands for development, security and operations. It's basically a framework to integrate the security factor in every phase of a software development cycle and its operations. Instead of functioning as a protective wall around the software or applications by the use of DevSecOps framework developers will be able to incorporate improved security measures in the development phases. DevSecOps provides the speed and agility of a DevOps framework along with improved security.


Why is a DevSecOps framework necessary?

In the modern-day working procedures, everyone has a role in keeping the data safe. This includes even the third-party members. The present state of the industries demand for a transparent and accountable working culture. DevSecOps helps to provide this by accumulating the necessary people, tools and processes to incorporate improved security measures. DevSecOps teams tend to make better decisions related to the projects due to the integration of security. Thus, this will reduce the need for large scale fixes down the road of development and operations. A DevSecOps team ensures protection of newly introduced features and components.

In case of cloud deployments, the use of open-source software is increasing day by day. As a result, one needs to be very mindful while using such services as the open-source software are being constantly improved and are prone to hidden dangers. It is quite possible to mistake malwares for the original package files. This may lead to data breaches. DevSecOps helps the teams to leverage open-source packages while removing and identifying the malicious components. That’s why DevSecOps is so very important while working in the cloud.

In recent times, cybersecurity breaches tend to plague the reputation of companies in almost every field. By using a DevSecOps framework, modified security ensures protection at every level in the development and operations cycle.


In a nutshell the advantages of a DevSecOps network are as follows -

• Automation of various tasks and monitoring for potential security threats throughout the development cycle.

• Customization of security parameters to detect the levels of the potential security breaches and data leakage.

• Coordinated task management.

• Reporting and remediation of potential security threats upon detection.

Considering all these advantages it is important to shift from the traditional DevOps to DevSecOps.


DevSecOps V/S DevOps

DevOps has considerably improved the software development process by automating tasks and boosting teamwork between the developers and operations team.However in DevOps security needs are often left out.This may lead to potential data breaches and data leakage. In case of DevSecOps security is incorporated in every phase while ensuring high standard performance. DevSecOps balances the equation and reduces the security constraints. It integrates security in the build, test, support and maintenance phases. This guarantees timely migration of fully secured data packages over the cloud. DevSecOps provides protection against cybersecurity breaches. DevSecOps is just a notch higher than DevOps.

With the inclination towards cloud-based services in order to boost a remote workforce the need for security in the predominant DevOps culture cannot be overlooked. So, it's about time that we adopt the DevSecOps framework.
