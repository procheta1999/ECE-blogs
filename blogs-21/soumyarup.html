AUGMENTED REALITY (AR)


Soumyarup Basu

Augmented reality is a part of immersive technology that can be considered as a blessing in our daily life because it helps us improve our real-life experiences with the detailed description they are capable of providing.

Augmented reality presents computer-generated augmentation on our present reality which helps us perform tasks better and with efficiency. Augmented reality acts as a bridge between virtual reality and our real world, rendering and mixing up data and parameters from both sections with high efficiency.

The development of the AR technology is not something new; it originated in 1968 on which professor Ivan Sutherland built the first AR device “The sword of Damocles” which used computer graphics wires to form drawings.

The utmost capabilities of the present-day AR technology have been presented to us in the form of a game Pokémon Go Developed by Niantic which has rendered the real-world data to the virtual world of the Pokémon giving us a feeling as if the Pokémon are roaming around us or the poke gym is located near me. This was an example where the real-world information is hired for the virtual world but in the case of google map, the case is almost the opposite where AR technology is used to show us information about a place or are virtually by presenting it in the real world of ours helping us to know more a place or to find a place with ease. AR helps brands provide a unique experience to the customer by providing detailed information about a particular product reducing the difference between purchase and dropout.

The hardware requirements of augmented reality are not something out of access which includes processor display sensors and input devices and display systems attached to the human body. The best use of AR is the helmet-mounted display which must be worn on the forehead. It places the virtual object in the real world in the user's field of view. Specific providers like uSnsen and gestures include gesture controls for full virtual immersions. The project of the use of AR in contact lenses is in progress. The first working prototype of the AR lens with no use of glasses was developed by Mojo visions in 2020.

The advancement in AR is also known as AR 2.0 which is the blending of the real world with the virtual world. Unlike AR, users can effectively interact with the virtual objects and place them in the desired place or give a shape of their own.AR2.0 is also known as mixed reality (MR). The future of AR 2.0 is not so distant and large companies knowing this fact invested up to 4billion dollars on AR and MR. It has been used widely in surgery, sports, construction, and design. The most famous MR design which is now present is the Microsoft hololens which is a pair of MR programmed glasses that allows us to experience 3D holograms blended into our real environment which allows us to interact with the models and get all the detailed information we need.

Hope this innovation will reach its peak in the coming days, and we will be able to obtain the maximum benefit from it.
